About DATASPHERE 26

Datasphere 26 is a National Level 24-hour Datathon bringing together innovators, developers, data analysts, AI enthusiasts, and students from across the country to solve real-world problems using Data Analytics, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, Business Intelligence, Generative AI, and Agentic AI.

Participants collaborate, innovate, and transform raw data into high-impact visual dashboards, diagnostic indicators, and intelligent agentic actions through intensive engineering, rapid iteration, and teamwork.

Presentation & Q&A

Information Help

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Datasphere 26?

+

Datasphere 26 is Sathyabama Institute's flagship National Level 24-hour Datathon. It is designed to challenge developers and AI enthusiasts to solve critical real-world business problems using data architecture, machine learning model fitting, and agentic workflows.

Who can participate?

+

The datathon is open to undergraduate, postgraduate, and engineering students across India who are passionate about data science, AI, modeling, and coding.

How many members are in a team?

+

Teams must consist of exactly 2 to 3 members. Individual registrations are not allowed, but you can form cohorts with others prior to registering.

Can we use ChatGPT?

+

You can use LLM models and assistive tools to aid coding, documentation, and debug cycles. However, copying pre-written functional systems or copying tracks from external creators directly is strictly prohibited and audited by plagiarism checks.

Can we use pre-trained models?

+

Yes, pre-trained base models from open repositories (like Hugging Face or PyTorch Hub) are fully allowed. Fine-tuning models or adding retrieval-augmented mechanisms (RAG) are highly encouraged.

Can we use external datasets?

+

No, you must construct predictions and dashboard insights based solely on the official datasets distributed at commencement. External supplemental datasets can only be used if specified in the problem statement.

What should we bring?

+

You must bring your own laptops, necessary adapters, ethernet cables, custom software dependencies set up in advance, and identification documents. Power outlets and standard campus internet nodes will be supplied.
